Checking a public sector employee's identity

Updated 9 February 2018

Contents

Overview

The new process for checking a public sector employee's identity has been designed to be more secure and require less work from both identity checkers and people whose identity is being checked.

The system uses:

  • GOV.UK Verify, the government standard for online identity verification
  • Live photo matching to the UK passport database
  • Additional document checking for people without a UK passport

Your responsibility as an ID checker

The following steps outline how to use the employee identity checking service.

You'll need to navigate to the service using the browser on a device which has a camera with at least 10 megapixels, such as a modern smartphone.

Step 1: The person being checked generates a code

Before you check someone's identity, they will need to generate a unique identity code using GOV.UK Verify.

When recruting new employees, a link to generate this code will be sent with their offer to interview.

Step 2: Ask the person for their code

When you meet the person face to face, you must first ask them for their code. They should be expecting to be asked for this.

You'll input the code into the employee identity checking service. The code should be 6 characters long and contain numbers only. e.g 653215.

If the person has no code, then you'll click the link saying "Candidate has no code" and continue to the next step.

Step 3: Take a photo of the person

You'll then need to take a photo of the person. The service will give you guidance about the quality of the image. You can also read the guidance on taking photos here.

You may need to do an additional step

If the person does not have a UK passport, the system will also prompt you to photograph their photo ID. This must be a UK driving license or a non-UK passport with a biometric residence permit.

If the person has not been able to verify their identity online, the system will also ask you to photograph their proof of address. This must be a bank statement, utilities bill, or proof of tenancy.

Final step: The result

When there are no more checks to complete, you will see a confirmation screen.

If the identity check fails, you won't find out immediately. You will receive an email with instructions with what you need to do.
