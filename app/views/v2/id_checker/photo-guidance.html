Rules for taking digital photos of applicants

Make sure the photo meets these rules. If it doesn’t, you may have problems with the application.

You can use a phone, tablet or digital camera.

What the digital photo must show

You'll need to fit the person's head, shoulders and upper body into the photo. Don’t crop your photo too tightly.

The photo must:

  • be taken against a plain, light-coloured background
  • contain no other objects or people
  • not have any shadows on the person's face or behind them

In the photo the person must:

  • be facing forward and looking straight at the camera
  • have a plain expression and mouth closed
  • have their eyes open and visible
  • not have hair covering their eyes
  • not wear anything on their head (unless it’s for religious or medical reasons)

The person must not wear sunglasses or tinted glasses. If they wear glasses that they can’t take off, their eyes must be visible without any glare or reflections.

The quality of the digital photo

The photo must be:

  • in focus and without any red eye
  • in colour with no effects or filters
  • at least 600 pixels wide and 750 pixels tall
  • at least 50kb and no more than 10mb
